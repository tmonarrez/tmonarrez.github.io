abstract: 'We study the causal effect of income-driven repayment (IDR) payment burdens on loan repayment and attainment for cohorts of first-time IDR applicants, using a regression discontinuity design based on payment thresholds. We find that zero payment requirements produce short-run reductions in delinquency and default risk, primarily by lowering the costs of inattention. However, these effects fade over time as some borrowers become disconnected from the repayment system. Our findings highlight the central role of nonfinancial frictions in shaping borrower outcomes under IDR.'
