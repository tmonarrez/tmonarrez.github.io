abstract: 'We study how local government boundaries contribute to racial residential segregation in the United States. Using census block data from 1990 to 2020 across all metropolitan areas, we find that segregation between jurisdictions represents a substantial share of overall racial stratification and has changed little over the past thirty years. Applying spatial regression discontinuity methods, we show that demographic discontinuities at jurisdiction boundaries cannot be fully explained by proximity to amenities, pointing to a role for local government policies. We document connections between cross-jurisdictional segregation and racial disparities in educational attainment.'
