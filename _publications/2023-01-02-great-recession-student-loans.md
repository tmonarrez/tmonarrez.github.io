abstract: 'We study the long-run effects of the Great Recession on federal student loan borrowing and repayment. Using longitudinal student loan data and variation in local labor market conditions during the recession, we find that a one percentage point increase in unemployment rates caused a 7 percent rise in total outstanding debt and a 6 percent rise in defaulted borrowers. The recession accounts for 19 to 32 percent of undergraduate debt increases and 10 to 25 percent of default increases across institutional sectors. Students enrolled at the onset of the recession experienced the largest effects, driven by delayed graduation and extended enrollment periods.'
